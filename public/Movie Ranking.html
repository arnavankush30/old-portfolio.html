Best Movies According to Arnav

My Top 3 Movies Of All times

  1. Three Idiots

    Directed by Rajkumar Hirani, this comedy-drama is based on Chetan Bhagat's novel "Five Point Someone." It follows the journey of three engineering students at an Indian engineering college and explores the pressures of the education system and societal expectations. It's not just hilarious but also delivers powerful messages about pursuing one's passion and questioning traditional norms.

  2. Taare Zameen Par

    Directed by Aamir Khan, this emotional drama tells the story of an eight-year-old dyslexic child who struggles with academics and is misunderstood by his parents and teachers. The film beautifully portrays the bond between the child and his art teacher who helps him discover his true potential. It's a heartwarming tale that sheds light on the importance of understanding and nurturing children's unique talents.

  3. Super 30

    Directed by Vikas Bahl, "Super 30" is a biographical drama inspired by the life of mathematician Anand Kumar, who coaches underprivileged students for the IIT entrance exams. The film celebrates the triumph of the underdog, addresses social issues, and highlights the transformative power of education. With its inspiring story and compelling performances, "Super 30" is a film that leaves a lasting impact on viewers.
